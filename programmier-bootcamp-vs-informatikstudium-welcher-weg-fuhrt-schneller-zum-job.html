Want a job in tech fast? Choosing between a coding bootcamp and a computer science degree is a big decision. Both paths lead to great careers, but which one gets you there quicker?

Bootcamps: The Fast Track

Bootcamps are intense. Think short, focused training. You'll learn practical skills, building projects for your portfolio. Perfect for quick entry into the job market. But, they might lack the theoretical depth of a degree.

Degrees: The Deep Dive

A computer science degree provides a strong foundation. You'll learn the theory behind programming, giving you a broader understanding. This can be beneficial for long-term career growth. However, it takes longer to complete, delaying your entry into the workforce.

Which Path is Right for You?

It depends on your goals and learning style. Need a job ASAP? A bootcamp might be your best bet. Want a more comprehensive education? A degree is the way to go. Consider your financial situation too. Bootcamps are often more affordable, but degrees can lead to higher earning potential in the long run.

My Experience

I know someone who went through a bootcamp and landed a job within months. Another friend is finishing their degree and is already getting internship offers. Both routes work!
